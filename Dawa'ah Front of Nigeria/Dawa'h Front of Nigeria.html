Welcome to Dawah Front of Nigeria

A modest response to the spiritual yearning and felt gap in the development of Muslims in our environment. We strive to provide accessible avenues for learning and practicing Islam in an easy-to-know manner.

With a motto – "Islam is Simple"; a vision to be a leading propagator of Islam as a simple, attractive and accessible way of life; and a mission to promote Islamic brotherhood, life fulfilment and excellence in Da’wah activities.

About Us

Da’wah Front of Nigeria was established in 1996 in response to the pressure to bridge the gap, provide felt needs, and create opportune avenues for learning and practice of Islam in an easy-to-know manner. Our first objective of spiritual liberation was felt achievable through the institution of monthly Tahajjud vide Qur’an 17:78-81 and Qur’an 73:1-8. Alhamdulillaah, today ample private and public testimonies of Allaah’s promise and fulfilment and the spread of this command throughout the Southwest Nigeria and even beyond justify our hopes of reward from Allaah.

Since inception, Da’wah Front has worked towards the education and re-education of Muslims, especially the Muslim elites, and we also tried to present Islam in its best form to non-Muslims.

Da’wah Front of Nigeria is registered with the Corporate Affairs Commission, and is composed of:

  • The Board of Trustees (BOT) – The Apex Organ;
  • The National Executive Council – The Administrative Organ;
  • The Chapter Executive Council – The Township Administrative Organ;
  • The Axis Executive Council – The Local Administrative Organ; and
  • The National General Meeting – The Overall Decision Ratifying Body.

What We Do

Our Activities Include:

  • Weekly Adhkaar on Sundays which features:
    • Family Seminar;
    • Lectures;
    • Counselling;
    • Da’wah workshop;
    • Academic and spiritual lessons for the children; and
    • Special prayers on the 5th Sundays of the month.
  • Weekly Da’wah on Television and Radio Stations across the country anchored by different Chapters and Axes of Da’wah Front of Nigeria.
  • Weekly women programme at the “Ummu Forum” on Saturdays.
  • Biannual youth programme on counselling, intellectual and spiritual development of our youths.
  • A monthly non-interest Cooperative programme at **Da’wah Front Multi-purpose Cooperative Society (DFMCS)**.
  • Yearly holiday programme tagged “Daura”.
  • Model nursery, primary and secondary schools across Southwest Nigeria.

National Programmes:

These are major initiatives designed for broader spiritual and intellectual development.

NATIONAL MUKHAYYAM:

This is a 3-day weekend programme which holds quarterly, four times in every Hijrah calendar year. It features topical issues and discussion projects around a chosen theme. Selected participants visit Raatib Mosques around the venue of the programme to give Da’wah and invite people. The essence is to uplift, train and develop the spiritual and intellectual wellbeing of members and non-members, challenging them to rise to their duties and responsibilities as good Muslims. Attendees are expected to inculcate what has been taught and also teach others. The venue is rotated amongst Chapters.

NATIONAL FAMILY SEMINAR:

This programme holds alongside National Mukhayyam especially on Saturday mornings between the hours of 10am and Dhuhr prayers. The purpose is to identify and address issues and challenges facing Muslim families in their marriages, homes and workplaces, providing solutions within the Absolute Reference Frame (Qur’aan and Sunnah).

DAURA:

This is an annual holiday camping programme that holds in the month of December in every Gregorian calendar year, usually designed to coincide with Christmas celebrations. It has held since inception at Salawu Abiola Comprehensive High School, Osiele, Abeokuta. It is open to members and non-members, offering opportunities for youths, children, and parents to congregate for educational, spiritual, and social development activities. It is also flavoured with career counseling, various self-employment schemes, and entrepreneurial skill development for integrated success laced with the fear of Allah.

Capital Projects

DA’WAH HOUSE:

This is the National Headquarters of Da’wah Front of Nigeria situated in Abeokuta. It remains uncompleted due to paucity of funds, and we welcome contributions towards its completion to further our mission.

EDUCATIONAL FACILITIES:

  • Front Foundation College, Abeokuta: Located in Sokoto Town in the outskirts of Abeokuta, Ogun State Capital City. It is a co-educational secondary school with boarding facilities. Although the school has existed since 2008 and is presently located on its permanent site, a lot needs to be done in terms of infrastructural facilities.
  • Front College, Ibadan: This school was established in 200- (year to be confirmed). It is located at Agric Bus Stop, Monatan, Ibadan and also a co-education secondary school, though presently without boarding facilities. The infrastructural facilities are gradually getting developed.
  • Front Nursery and Primary School, Ibadan: This school, also co-educational, has two (2) branches within Ibadan Metropolis. One is located in Bashorun Area of Ibadan and the other is located at Agric Bus Stop, Monatan, Ibadan.

These schools were established to provide qualitative education for Muslim children with the aims of raising Muslim personalities who would be intellectually and spiritually balanced, socially and morally sound, and imbued with the fear of Allah.

DA’WAH FRONT MULTIPURPOSE COOPERATIVE SOCIETY (DFMCS):

This is a Zero-interest based cooperative society established by Da’wah Front of Nigeria in October, 2001 where funds are created and lent to its members for general business ventures in accordance with Islamic injunctions. The following are some of the objectives of the cooperative society:

  • The creation of funds to be lent to its members for necessary economic/productive purpose;
  • The provision of facilities for regular thrift savings by members, without such being removed from their control;
  • To invest and deal with money of the society in such a manner as may from time to time be determined by the management committee of the society;
  • To carry out the business of manufacturing and distribution of consumable items; and
  • To carry out the business of agriculture through mechanised farming, poultry, livestock, agro-allied products etc.

The Society’s products include: Thrift and Credit Loan; Hajj Savings Scheme; Education Savings Scheme; Health Savings Scheme; Investment Windows; Asset Acquisition Scheme; Ileya Savings Scheme; and Consultancy Services on Zero interest-based Cooperative.

Quick Questions (FAQ)

What is Da’wah Front of Nigeria?

Da’wah Front of Nigeria is an organization established in 1996 to address the spiritual and developmental needs of Muslims, providing avenues for learning and practicing Islam in an easy-to-understand manner. Our motto is "Islam is Simple."

What are your main activities?

Our activities include weekly Adhkaar sessions, TV and radio Dawah broadcasts, women's programmes, biannual youth programmes, a non-interest cooperative society (DFMCS), annual holiday camps ("Daura"), and running model schools. We also hold National Mukhayyam and Family Seminars quarterly.

How can I support Dawah Front of Nigeria?

You can support us through members' monthly subscriptions, voluntary contributions, and occasional donations. We also finance projects through Islamic investment windows. Donations can be made via cash, cheque, or direct/online bank transfer to our Guaranty Trust Bank account (details in the Contact & Support section).
